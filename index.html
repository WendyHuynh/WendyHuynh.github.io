Janson Equipment

Janson Equipment wanted a website that they had control over to make changes in the future. What resulted was a content management system site that allowed them just that. Aside from equipment, communication was an important feature Janson Equipment wanted. They wanted every opportunity for customers to reach them through social media, sign up for the newsletter or hear how they helped other customers. Project responsibilities began with a mockup done in Photoshop after the initial consultation to a fully coded and functional site coded with PHP and Silverstripe.

Used Krone North America

Used Krone North America has been one of the most complex sites I've created. I took over this project after approval of analysis and built this site with HTML5, CSS3, and Javascript. Inventory was their number one concern and they wanted users to be able to search listings, not only by category, but by price, year, etc. Being a dealer group, the listings they wanted called in were very specific using dealer showrooms to filter new inventory.

Bobcat Plus

Bobcat Plus was an easier site in a design sense since they are part of a widely known brand that they wanted represented in their website. After a consultation with the customer, I created an analysis in Photoshop and built the site with HTML5 and CSS3. It was very important to this customer for their listings to be easily accessed and categorized efficiently for users with a dealer showroom and frameless inventory. Not only was the look and feel of the site very similar to other Bobcat sites, the functionality also translated to Bobcat Plus.

Truck King International

Truck King International was a sister site to Salem Trucks, both of which I created analyses and built with HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript. With this task, I had to make two very similar, but standalone websites. I worked with JavaScript to create a flip animation to the call out buttons underneath the slider. When hovering over sales for example, it flips to a more in depth description of what will be found in that tab along with a link to navigate to the page. This site has not been rolled out live.

Wickliff Auctioneers

Wickliff Auctioneers is the most recent CMS site I created. Their main purpose was to advertise upcoming auctions and take customers right to the listing. With their auctions changing on a regular basis, I setup a user friendly system to add and remove auctions, blogs, and other content. The most difficult section of the site creation was the blog. Their blog section was very in depth and dated back to 2012. After creating a template using PHP files with Silverstripe, what resulted were simple to follow fields for the customer to plug in information. This site has not been rolled out live.
